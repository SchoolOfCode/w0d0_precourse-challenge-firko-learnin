My Morning Latte Routine

*My latte art never looks remotely like this.

Ingredients:

  • 18g of freshly roasted coffee beans
  • Whole or gold top milk

Equipment:

  • Coffee grinder and grind cup
  • WDT tool
  • Distribution tool
  • Tamping mat
  • Espresso machine
  • Bottomless portafilter and funnel
  • Double shot non-pressurised basket
  • 0.1g espresso scale with timer
  • Two mugs
  • Milk jug
  • Milk thermometer or colour changing sticker
  • Wet cloth

Steps:

  1. Turn on the espresso machine and ensure sufficient water is available in the tank.
  2. Place an empty mug under the group head and run 1-2 blank shots to heat up the machine.
  3. Using the espresso scale, weigh out 17-18g of freshly roasted coffee beans.
  4. Add the coffee beans to the grinder and grind at the relevant setting, ensuring the grind cup is under the grinder.
  5. Give the grounds a shake and using the funnel add the grounds to the portafilterm, working over the tamping mat to catch any runaway grounds.
  6. Using the WDT distribute the grounds evenly and give the portafilter a tap before removing the funnel.
  7. Distribute the grounds using the distribution side of the tool then flip the tool to tamp.
  8. Lock the portafilter into the group head until tight.
  9. Place the espresso scale on the drip tray and place a clean mug on the scale then zero.
  10. Start the timer on the scale and begin extracting coffee using the machine's manual mode, aiming for an extraction time of 25-30 seconds and an output of 34-36g (or to taste).
  11. Once the coffee has extracted remove the cup, scale and portafilter from the machine, discarding the used grounds.
  12. Shake the milk bottle and fill the milk jug just below the start of the spout.
  13. Purge the steam wand for a few seconds to heat up.
  14. Place the milk jug under the steam wand and aerate (with a ripping paper sound) with the wand tip half submerged for 5-8 seconds whilst maintaining a whirlpool motion.
  15. Raise the milk jug to fully submerge the wand tip and heat until the milk reaches 60°C.
  16. Turn off the steam wand, wipe down and purge for a few seconds.
  17. Swirl the milk in the jug to help incorporate the foam then tap on a surface to remove bubbles.
  18. Pour really bad latte art, photographing any particularly bad attempts to share with friends:
  19. Drink coffee.
